Galileo Galilei

Father of Modern Science

Galilei is known for discovering the four moons of Jupiter along with the rings of Saturn, among many other things.

    Here's a time line of Galileo Galilei's life:
  • 1564 - Born in Pisa, Italy
  • 1581 - Began studies in medicine at the University of Pisa, but soon switched to study mathematics
  • 1583 - First major discovery, explains motion of pendulums
  • 1589 - Becomes chair of mathematics at the University of Pisa
  • 1592 - Moves to the University of Padua, continues to teach astronomy, mechanics, and geometry
  • 1609 - Builds his own telescope
  • 1610 - Discovers four objects orbiting Jupiter, turns out to be the four largest moons of Jupiter
  • 1610 - Publishes Sidereus Nuncius to explain his discoveries found with his telescope, becomes widely celebrated in Italy
  • 1613 - Galilei writes letters defending his views on heliocentricism
  • 1616 - His writings are officially banned by the Catholic Church
  • 1623 - Publishes The Assayer where he explains the scientific method along with showing how to read nature by using math
  • 1632 - Publishes Dialogo sopra i due massimi sistemi del mondo, a book comparing the two views of the universe - the Copernican system and the Ptolemaic system through given permission by the Inquisition
  • 1633 - Galilei is sent to face the Roman Inquisition for his views and writings and is placed under house arrest for 9 years.
  • 1638 - Publishes his final book while in house arrest, Discorsi e Dimostrazioni Matematiche Intorno a Due Nuove Scienze, to explain his many years of physics research
  • 1642 - Dies at the age of 77

    • “Galileo, perhaps more than any other single person, was responsible for the birth of modern science.”

    -Stephen Hawking
